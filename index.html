The Client

The client is a medical device packaging manufacturer with more than three decades of experience producing sterile procedure trays, surgical kits, and disposable medical instruments used by hospitals and ambulatory surgery centers.

Despite a strong reputation and an extensive product catalog, the company relied primarily on referrals and existing customer relationships to generate new business.

Target Audience

The campaign targeted operating room materials managers in ambulatory surgery centers and hospitals, particularly facilities performing high volumes of orthopedic joint replacement procedures.

These professionals are responsible for selecting surgical kits, sterile trays, and procedure packs used by surgical teams.

The Problem

While the company had a highly competitive portfolio of sterile surgical kits and procedure trays, awareness among hospital purchasing teams remained limited.

The organization lacked a scalable marketing system and relied heavily on inbound referrals.

Without a structured outbound process, the sales team had limited capacity to consistently generate new opportunities in the market.

The Mechanism

Hyper-Targeted Outreach
Lucid Strategies built a highly targeted prospect database of hospital decision-makers.

Multi-Channel Engagement
Prospects were contacted through coordinated outreach campaigns combining cold calls, email communication, LinkedIn engagement, and SMS follow-ups.

Structured Outreach Cadence
Each prospect entered a structured outreach cadence of up to 555 touches across multiple channels.

Free Sample Program
A product sample program allowed qualified hospitals and surgery centers to evaluate sterile procedure kits directly within their clinical workflow.

The Result
Within two months the number of warm leads increased from 3 to 10, representing a 233% increase in qualified engagement from hospital decision-makers.

The campaign generated strong market validation, producing dozens of email information requests and product sample requests from qualified surgical facilities.

These interactions confirmed that hospitals were actively exploring supplier alternatives once they became aware of the client's offering.

Definitions

Email Information Request
Occurs when a hospital decision-maker expresses interest during outreach and requests additional product information via email.

Sample Request
Occurs when a qualified healthcare professional requests a physical product sample to evaluate the product within their facility.
